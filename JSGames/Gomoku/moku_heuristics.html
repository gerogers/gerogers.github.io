Gomoku - Heuristics - the learning process

Return to index. To leave help, click on the Help drop-down menu.

This implementation is strictly heuristic. There is no look ahead. Moves are based on the recognition of patterns encountered in earlier play or previous games. The program's prime goal is to achieve the 5-in-a-row pattern. In the situation where this is the only goal, a loss implies that the move just before the winning move (at a critical node) must have established at least two separate patterns each of which needs defending. The program identifies those two patterns and generates a description of the two patterns combined by the critical node. This pattern description becomes a new sub-goal. In subsequent plays, situations arise where 2 sub-goals are realised simultaneously (and so can't be defended). A new sub-goal is then generated by combining those earlier sub-goals through a critical node.

The program attempts to generate pattern descriptions that are as general as possible. Thus the description A4 means: "there exists some node A, such that after playing at A it will be part of at least 2 potential 5-patterns in each of which 4 pieces (of the same colour) have been played. The line through node A may lie in any orientation."

A fuller description of the pattern description is given in the section entitled " What do the sub-goal descriptions mean?"
